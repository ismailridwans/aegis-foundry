THE PROBLEM

The broken factory.

Enterprises run hundreds of correlation searches. A large fraction are stale, broken by schema drift, or were never tuned to the environment they run in.

Covering a new threat technique takes days to weeks — every rule needs authoring, historical validation, false-positive tuning, and change control. All of it manual.

Alert fatigue is a symptom, not the disease. Every triage copilot mops the floor a little faster. Nobody fixed the faucet. Aegis Foundry fixes the faucet.

WHY IT WINS

Six unfair advantages.

Forecast-gated deployment

The Cisco Deep Time Series Model prices a rule's alert noise before it reaches production. Over budget means it does not ship.

Backtested and red-teamed

Every detection is replayed over 90 days of labeled history, then a Red-Team agent mutates those attacks into evasion variants to prove it resists an adversary — not just the past.

Evidence-pack governance

SPL diff, backtest table, forecast verdict and 7 policy checks — bundled into one pack a human approves, shadows, or rejects.

Tamper-evident flight recorder

Every agent action lands in a SHA-256 hash-chained, Splunk-ingestible audit log — edit any past entry and the chain breaks. The swarm is observable and provable in one pane of glass.

ATT&CK forge

Coverage gaps close themselves: the heatmap cell flips from GAP to FORGED BY AEGIS the moment the rule deploys.

Runs anywhere

Splunk Cloud hosted models — or fully air-gapped open weights. The EWMA fallback labels itself honestly in every artifact.
